What is a plagiarism checker?

A plagiarism checker scans your text against a large database of sources—including the web, academic papers, and publications—to find matches and similarity. It helps you ensure your work is original and properly cited.

Tips to avoid plagiarism

Best practices for keeping your work original and correctly cited:

Cite your sources

When you use someone else's ideas or words, add in-text citations and a reference list. Our checker helps you find missing citations so you can fix them before submission.

Paraphrase properly

Paraphrasing means rewriting in your own words and structure—not just swapping a few words. Use aiwriting's writing and rewriting tools to help rephrase while keeping meaning clear.

Check early and often

Run a plagiarism check on drafts as you go, not only at the end. Catching issues early makes it easier to revise and cite correctly.

Verify after humanizing or rewriting

If you use an AI humanizer or other rewriting tools, run the plagiarism checker again to ensure the final text is still original and properly attributed.
